Courses I'm taking this semester

15-316: Software Foundations of Security and Privacy

This class is my favorite of of all the courses I am taking right now. I was introduced to some of the concepts taught in the class like information flow and side channels from the intro course I took last semester. This class goes into much more detail and introduces how safety policies addressing these issues can be formalized. It has been very interesting to see how we can matematically prove that the program we write will not be vulnerable to a certain class of bugs.

15-451: Algorithm Design and Analysis

After going through 15-251 and 15-210, the theoretical CS and algorithm courses at CMU, I was fully expecting this course to be nearly impossible. To my surprise, I was able to understand most of the concepts after hearing it from lecture. The course is difficult, but the foundations provided to me have prepared me well.

The course starts put by giving us a framework for evaluating algorithms, and taught us to ask quesitons such as, how close to the optimal will our algorithm's output be? Is there room for improvement, or does the algorithm reach a lower bound in terms of space or time? The next part of the course focused on algorithms and data structures for common problems in CS such as using Splay-Trees for slightly faster guarantees when the access pattern of the inputs are known and pushing the maximum amount of flow. 17-333: Privacy,Policy, Technology and Law

I have to admit, this was a required course for the security and privacy concentration, so I didn't come in with the highest expectations. The course description described the course as more of a policy one, and would discuss current privacy laws and their motives. Initially, I was unconvinced that policies were a viable solution, seeing that larger companies like Google and Facebook have been harvesting data for a while now even with these laws. After the first few weeks however, I changed my stance. The course so far has really shown me that policy can be effective, as things like the GDPR and CCPA are forcing companies to actually respect consumer privacy. Additionally, while many issues like web tracking and data aggregation remain prevalent, it is only because there are studies attempting to figure out a way to adress these compelx issues that is both enforcable and will not bankrupt any businesses. Secureum Bootcamp

I am currently participating in the Secureum Bootcamp to better understand smart contract vulnerabilities and how to audit contracts to find such vulnerabilities. The bootcamp is loosely structured, giving all participants weekly materials to read as well as optional assignments to help strengthen understanding. So far, they have only covered Ethereum and Solidity, but have went into incredible detail on both. I have had experience writing smart contracts in Solidity, but have learned so much about the specifis of Solidity such as the bytecode instruciton used by the EVM as well as how memory and storage are managed. I am looking forward to the audit part of the bootcamp, as I'll finally get to exploit some vulnerabilites with my newfound knowledge. More details
