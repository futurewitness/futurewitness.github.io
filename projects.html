What I'm doing now

Courses I'm taking this semester

15-410: Operating Systems Desgin and Implementation

So far, this class is by far the most frustrating class I have taken in all of my years here at CMU. Everyone coming into this course knows what they're getting into. If the 28 FCE wasn't enough of a warning, the professors make that very clear on the syllabus. So far, each project has been very difficult, but very helpful in learning the associated concepts. Project 1 for example involved designing and implementing device drivers for a keyboard and screen. I have never thought about what happens between pressing a key on my keyboard and seeing a character on the screen, but through this project, I have a much better understanding of how interrupts and device drivers work together to bridge the gap between hardware and software.

15-435: Foundations of Blockchains

This is one of the more intersting courses I have taken here at CMU. The professor of the course, Elaine Shi is very well known in the blockchain space, so getting to take a course from her is a unique opportunity for me. It is very well taught, teaching us the important parts of Cryptography and Distributed Systems required to fully understand where a blockchain gets it's properties from. Course Site

42-202: Physiology

I have to admit, this course fulfills a general requirement. That does not mean it isn't worth my time, as the material covered in class is very applicable for real world scenarios. I can definitely see myself using my knowledge of the muscular system to know when I might need to get a blood test when I otherwise might not need one. Previous Course Sites
